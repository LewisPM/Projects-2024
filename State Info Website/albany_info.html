Notoriety

Albany is the capital and the oldest city of New York State. Because of this it is know for its historic architecture, urban parks and landmarks.

Information

Albany is an urban area totalling 22 sq miles/ 57 sq kilometers, located Center East in New York State.

The Mohican tribe of Native Americans were settled in the area and traded fur with the Dutch and English as early as 1540, but claims to the land by Europeans did not happen until 1609. In 1614 a trading post was built by a Dutchman which started conflit with the French and Indians who vied to control fur-trade. After disputes and civil wars, in 1664 England reclaimed the area and named it Albany after James II, Duke of Albany. 1

The estimated total population as of 2023 is 101,228. 2

The estimated average yearly income is $64,720 (compared to $62,960 average in the U.S.). 3

Albany has the earliest celebration of Santa Claus - a mainstay in American Christmas tradition. It was on a bakery receipt March of 1675.
February, 18, 2021, Sophie Nadeau, "Amazing, Weird, Fun & Interesting Facts About Albany, New York"

History


By City of Albany New York - Public Domain

The Erie Canal lead to an economic boom by increasing the connectivity of various settlements. Albany Hosts the Erie Canal Bicentennial celebrating Erie Canal and Albany's history. 4

As the state capital, Albany provides a central location for the state's administrative, legislative and sometimes judicial branches.
