Notoriety

Albany is the capital and the oldest city of New York State. Because of this it is know for its historic architecture, urban parks and landmarks, sports, winters and its iconic food - Buffalo Wings.

Information

Albany is an urban area totalling 22 sq miles/ 57 sq kilometers, located Center East in New York State.

The Mohawk tribe of Native Americans traded fur with the Dutch and English as early as 1540, but claims to the land by Europeans did not happen until 1609. In 1614 a trading post was built by a Dutchman which started conflit with the French and Indians who vied to control fur-trade. After disputes and civil wars, in 1664 England reclaimed the area and named it New York after James II, Duke of Albany. 1

The estimated total population as of 2023 is 101,228. 2

The estimated average yearly income is $64,720 (compared to $62,960 average in the U.S.). 3

History

From the late 18th to the late 19th century, New York City started resemble what it does today. The Erie Canal lead to an economic boom by increasing the connectivity of various settlements. Many notable companys also started around this time such as American Express, Chase, At&T, IBM, Pfizer, Macy's, General Electric, Goldman Sachs and others. New York City was the largest port of entry for legal immigration. Ellis Island was opened January 1st 1892 and stopped immigration processing November 12th 1954.

More than 12 million immigrants passed through Ellis Island between 1892 and 1954. More than 100 million Americans across the United States can trace their ancestry to these immigrants.
