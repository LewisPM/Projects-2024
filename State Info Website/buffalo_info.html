Buffalo

Notoriety

Buffalo is know for its historic architecture, urban parks and landmarks, sports, winters and its iconic food - Buffalo Wings.

Information

Buffalo is an urban area totalling 472 sq miles/ 1,224 sq kilometers, located South East of New York State.

In 1620 the French explored the area that was considered terriories for the Mohawk rtibe of Native Americans. A French settlement existed for a year in 1758. After the French and Indian War the region was ruled by Britain. New York and Massachusetts began to expand and both tried to claim the land. After sale and indian relocation around 1800, the area formally known as New Amsterdam was renamded Buffalo, after Buffalo Creek. 1

The estimated total population as of 2023 is 274,678. 2

The estimated average yearly income is $60,720 (compared to $62,960 average in the U.S.). 3

History


By City of Buffalo New York - Public Domain

In the mid 18th century, the Erie Canal was completed and lead to an economic boom by increasing the connectivity of various settlements. As the anti-slavery movement grew, Buffalo became a gathering place for Abolitionists and was a terminus point on the Underground Railroad. 4

Buffalo was the first city in the United States to install streetlights in 1886 due to the power produced by Niagra Falls.

Buffalo was an industrial town prospering from manufacturing and grain production. Resistance to modernization lead to a decline, losing 70,000 jobs between 1970 and 1984. Economic recovery from deinsurtialization was a focus for Buffalo in the late 20th century. 5
